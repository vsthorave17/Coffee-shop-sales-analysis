# Project Overview
This project analyzes Coffee Shop Sales performance using an interactive dashboard to uncover sales trends, customer behavior, and product performance. The dashboard provides a comprehensive view of total sales, footfall, ordering patterns, and category-wise performance across store locations, time, and order sizes to support data-driven business decisions.


# Problem Statement:
1) Peak sales hours and customer footfall trends.
2)Best-performing products, categories, and store locations.
3)Customer order size and purchasing behavior.
4)Sales variation across weekdays and time periods.

# Key Insights:

The dashboard reveals that the coffee shop generated $698,812.33 in total sales from a footfall of 149,116 customers, with an average bill value of 4.69 and 1.4 orders per person, indicating consistent but relatively small transaction sizes per visit. Order quantities peak sharply during the morning hours between 7 AM and 10 AM, highlighting breakfast time as the most critical revenue window. From a product perspective, Coffee accounts for the largest share of sales at 39%, followed by Tea at 28%, confirming that hot beverages are the primary revenue drivers, while bakery and specialty items add incremental value. Customer preferences show a strong inclination toward Regular (31%) and Large (30%) drink sizes, suggesting opportunities for size-based upselling. Among store locations, Hell’s Kitchen outperforms Astoria and Lower Manhattan in both sales and footfall, making it the top-performing outlet. Additionally, Barista Espresso and Brewed Chai Tea emerge as the highest-selling products, while Fridays record the highest weekday footfall, with comparatively lower demand observed on weekends, especially Saturdays.

# Business Recommendations:
Increase staffing and inventory during morning peak hours (7–10 AM).
Focus promotions on high-performing products like espresso and brewed beverages.
Introduce combos or upselling strategies for Regular and Large sizes.
Run weekday-focused offers to maximize already strong mid-week footfall.
Optimize product visibility in high-performing locations such as Hell’s Kitchen.
